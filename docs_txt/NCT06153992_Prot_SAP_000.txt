1 Materials and Methods


[Title] (page 3)
1.1 General information


[NarrativeText] (page 3)
A total of 90 stroke hemiplegic rehabilitation patients who were


[NarrativeText] (page 3)
hospitalized in the Rehabilitation Department of Qianfoshan Hospital in


[NarrativeText] (page 3)
Shandong Province from January 2022 to January 2023 were selected,


[NarrativeText] (page 3)
meeting the diagnostic criteria for cerebral


[Title] (page 3)
infarction and cerebral


[Title] (page 3)
hemorrhage in the 2007 edition of the Chinese Guidelines for the


[NarrativeText] (page 3)
Prevention and Treatment of Cerebrovascular Diseases, and confirmed by


[NarrativeText] (page 3)
cranial CT or MR examination; All of them are first-time onset, with a


[NarrativeText] (page 3)
course of ≤ 1 month, and the lower limb of the hemiplegic side is


[NarrativeText] (page 3)
classified as stage III or above. All participants signed an informed


[NarrativeText] (page 3)
consent form. All patients have stable vital signs, stable condition,


[Title] (page 3)
no history of congenital diseases or other brain diseases, no history of


[NarrativeText] (page 3)
organic or functional mental illness. Divide patients into a control group


[NarrativeText] (page 3)
and a treatment group using a random number table method. There are 30


[Title] (page 3)
cases in the control group, 30 cases in the intelligent isokinetic treatment


[Title] (page 3)
group, and 30 cases in the traditional isokinetic treatment group.


[Title] (page 3)
1.2 Method


[Title] (page 3)
Both the control group and the two treatment groups


[NarrativeText] (page 3)
received


[Title] (page 4)
minutes a time, 5 times a week), acupuncture and moxibustion therapy


[NarrativeText] (page 4)
(once a day, 20 minutes a time, 5 times a week), which complied with the


[Title] (page 4)
recommendations on rehabilitation treatment in the 2017 edition of the


[Title] (page 4)
Guidelines for Early Rehabilitation Treatment of Stroke in China;


[NarrativeText] (page 4)
Intelligent isokinetic therapy adds isokinetic muscle strength training to


[Title] (page 4)
the lower limb flexion and extension muscles of hemiplegia in the above


[Title] (page 4)
treatments. The intelligent portable isokinetic tester of this project team


[NarrativeText] (page 4)
was used for isokinetic training of the affected knee flexor and extensor


[NarrativeText] (page 4)
muscles. The patient's seat backrest was adjusted to 85 °, and the waist


[NarrativeText] (page 4)
and shoulder cross straps were fixed. The proximal thighs of the test


[NarrativeText] (page 4)
subjects were fixed with nylon buckles, the lateral condyle of the femur


[NarrativeText] (page 4)
was the axis, and the length of the arm was 12 on the scale. The distal


[NarrativeText] (page 4)
ankle of the lower leg was fixed above the ankle. Before training, passive


[NarrativeText] (page 4)
joint movements were performed on the knee and ankle joints to avoid


[NarrativeText] (page 4)
joint damage, And conduct


[Title] (page 4)
three low resistance warm-up exercises.


[NarrativeText] (page 4)
Choose 60 ° /s, 90 ° /s, and 120 ° /s angular velocity isokinetic


[NarrativeText] (page 4)
training based on the patient's specific situation. Train 10 times for each


[Title] (page 4)
angular velocity, with 15 seconds of rest between each cycle, and 2


[NarrativeText] (page 4)
minutes of rest between each cycle. Train for 4 cycles according to the


[NarrativeText] (page 4)
patient's tolerance, with an appropriate amount of training to cause


[NarrativeText] (page 5)
minutes per training session). Train each muscle group once a day, 5 days


[NarrativeText] (page 5)
a week, for a total of 3 weeks. The control group received 110 minutes of


[NarrativeText] (page 5)
daily training, while the observation group received 135 minutes of daily


[NarrativeText] (page 5)
training. Traditional isokinetic therapy has the same treatment parameters


[Title] (page 5)
as the intelligent isokinetic group in this project group.

1.3 Evaluation method


[NarrativeText] (page 5)
Before and after treatment, a dedicated person is assigned to assess


[NarrativeText] (page 5)
whether the patient is in the control group or treatment group, which is a


[NarrativeText] (page 5)
third-party blind assessment. Evaluate lower limb motor function using


[Title] (page 5)
the Fugl Meyer method (total lower limb motor score 34 points, total


[NarrativeText] (page 5)
balance score 14 points). Muscle strength, muscle tone examination,


[NarrativeText] (page 5)
motor function, and self-care ability scores. And record the peak torque


[NarrativeText] (page 5)
(PT) at an angular velocity of 60 °/s. The reason for choosing this angle


[NarrativeText] (page 5)
is because most of our subjects find it difficult


[NarrativeText] (page 5)
to produce faster


[NarrativeText] (page 5)
movements. PT refers to the maximum torque output generated by muscle


[NarrativeText] (page 5)
contraction, representing the maximum muscle force generated by muscle


[Title] (page 5)
contraction, in N ·m. In isokinetic muscle strength testing, the PT value


[NarrativeText] (page 5)
has high accuracy and repeatability, and is considered the golden


[NarrativeText] (page 5)
indicator of isokinetic muscle strength testing. It has high repeatability,


[NarrativeText] (page 5)
and the peak torque measurement of knee joint flexion and extension is


[Title] (page 5)
particularly stable. At the same time, a satisfaction questionnaire survey


[NarrativeText] (page 6)
was conducted on the use of different isokinetic methods, quantifying the


[NarrativeText] (page 6)
characteristics of the two types of isokinetic methods based on the results:


[NarrativeText] (page 6)
the survey content includes patients' comfort, satisfaction, and treatment


[NarrativeText] (page 6)
effectiveness; Therapist: Conduct statistics on operability, learning curve,


[Title] (page 6)
economy, and other aspects.

2 Statistical analysis


[NarrativeText] (page 6)
SPSS 23.0 software was used for data statistical analysis. The


[NarrativeText] (page 6)
measurement data is represented by the mean ± standard deviation, the


[NarrativeText] (page 6)
inter group comparison is performed using one-way analysis of variance,


[NarrativeText] (page 6)
the pairwise comparison between groups is performed using LSD test, the


[NarrativeText] (page 6)
mean comparison between two samples is performed using t-test, and the


[NarrativeText] (page 6)
rate comparison of categorical data is performed using chi square test.


[NarrativeText] (page 6)
P<0.05 indicates a statistically significant difference.

The investigators1 do not foresee any additional risks to subjects who participate in the study,


[NarrativeText] (page 2)
compared to the day-to-day standard therapy protocol, as the interventions will be


[NarrativeText] (page 2)
implemented in the same physical environments, with the same equipment and personnel.

[Title] (page 2)
Safety Measurements


[NarrativeText] (page 2)
In the event of any adverse event, the standard protocol will be implemented. Subjects’


[NarrativeText] (page 2)
confidentiality will be guarded by assigning IDs, which link to their names in a separate


[NarrativeText] (page 2)
password-protected document accessible via a password-protected user account in a laptop or


[NarrativeText] (page 2)
desktop, only accessed by the investigator. All physical documents will be locked in a


[NarrativeText] (page 2)
designated file cabinet in a locked office where only the investigator has the key. All


[NarrativeText] (page 2)
documents will be kept for two years before they are shredded. All subjects may raise any


[NarrativeText] (page 2)
at the facility. A phone number and an email address will be given to all subjects in the


[NarrativeText] (page 2)
consent form. They may also report to the person in charge of the ethics committee team,


[NarrativeText] (page 2)
whose contact information will be provided on the consent form as well.

[NarrativeText] (page 2)

[NarrativeText] (page 2)
capacity of the music therapist. It is intended for the data collection phase to not exceed 1


[NarrativeText] (page 2)
year. After data collection is complete for a minimum of 30 subjects, data analysis will begin.
